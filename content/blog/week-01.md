## 🎉 Day 1 — Arriving at the Office

The alarm went off at 5:30 AM. After months of waiting, my On-the-Job Training was finally starting. I dressed up in business casual, grabbed my laptop bag, and headed to the office — a mid-sized software company about 40 minutes from home.

The security guard handed me a visitor's badge and escorted me to the IT department. My supervisor, **Sir Mark**, welcomed me with a firm handshake and introduced me to the rest of the team: three senior developers, one QA engineer, and another intern named Carla who had started a week before me.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack Briefing

After the quick tour, Sir Mark sat down with me for an onboarding session. The team uses:

- **Vue 3 + Nuxt 3** for the frontend
- **Node.js + Express** for the backend API
- **PostgreSQL** for the database
- **Git (GitHub)** for version control
- **Jira** for task tracking

I was relieved — I had some experience with Vue from school projects. But Nuxt and PostgreSQL were new territory.

## 📋 Tasks This Week

1. Set up my local development environment (Node.js, VSCode, Git)
2. Clone the company's main project repository
3. Review existing codebase documentation
4. Attend a sprint planning meeting as an observer
5. Complete the company's internal onboarding quiz

## 😓 Challenges

Setting up the development environment took longer than expected. The project's `.env` file had missing configurations that weren't documented, and I had to ask Sir Mark twice for clarification without feeling like a burden. Imposter syndrome hit early.

The sprint planning meeting was overwhelming — acronyms flying everywhere: MVC, ORM, CI/CD. I furiously took notes while pretending to follow along.

## 💡 Lessons Learned

> **Always ask questions early.** Every day I hesitate to clarify something costs me hours of confusion later.

- Read the README *and* the contributing guide before touching any code
- Setting up a dev environment is a skill in itself — document every step
- Being the new person in a room full of experts is uncomfortable but essential for growth

## 🔜 Looking Ahead

Next week I'll be assigned my first real task: fixing a minor UI bug on the admin dashboard. I'm equal parts nervous and excited. Time to prove I belong here!
