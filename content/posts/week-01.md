## Overview

The first week of my OJT was both exciting and nerve-wracking. I reported to the company for the first time, met my supervisor and teammates, and began getting comfortable with the professional environment.

## Tasks Performed

- **Orientation & Onboarding** — Attended company orientation, reviewed company policies, and received my equipment.
- **Environment Setup** — Installed required tools: VS Code, Node.js, Git, and Docker.
- **Codebase Exploration** — Cloned the main project repository and spent time reading through the code structure and documentation.
- **First Task** — Fixed a minor UI bug as a warm-up task to get familiar with the workflow.

## Challenges Encountered

Getting used to the Git branching workflow was a bit challenging at first. The team uses a feature branch strategy with pull requests, which was different from how I worked in school projects.

> **How I solved it:** My supervisor walked me through the process and I practiced creating feature branches and opening PRs on a test repository.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Professional Git workflow** — Always branch off from `develop`, write clear commit messages, and open a pull request for review.
2. **Ask questions early** — It's better to clarify expectations upfront than to spend hours on the wrong direction.
3. **Read the docs first** — The team had thorough documentation that answered most of my initial questions.

## Reflection

Starting something new is always intimidating, but everyone on the team was welcoming and patient. I'm excited for the weeks ahead and motivated to contribute meaningfully to the projects.
